Rewriting sentences such that it emphasizes the specific adjective.

Given a sentence and a new piece of information, rewrite the sentence such that the new piece of information is included.

Note that the original sentences will be without punctuations, and the re-written sentences should also not have punctuations. Other than punctuation, the rewritten sentence should be grammatically correct and free of misspelled words

If incorporating the information is impossible or if the information is already included in the original sentence, write "N/A" as your answer.

After you rewrite the sentence, you need to review the sentence you wrote and determine if the sentence fits common sense, and check the "make sense" box accordingly.

For example, the sentence "The cat is small" with info "['cat','large']" will be rewritten as "The large cat is small", which is grammatical but does not fit common sense, so the make-sense box should not be checked.

There are a lot more examples of what should be done in the "examples" tab. Please view them before starting the task.
